eradicated

Appalachia Service Project or ASP is a Christian volunteer organization operating in Central Appalachia. Its primary goal is to make homes "warmer, safe, and drier".

ASP At a Glance

ASP was founded in 1969 by Rev. Tex Evans to make homes warmer, safer & drier.

Since then over 300,000 volunteers have repaired more than 15,000 homes.

On average, 16,000 volunteers from more than 30 states come to serve each year

In 2013, ASP will serve in 31 impoverished communities in KY, NC, TN, VA, and WV.

Founded by Methodists, ASP is a Christian mission, but is non-sectarian in all its programs.

ASP is only able to assist one of every five families who apply for help. Each year ASP must decline providing assistance to thousands of people who have nowhere else to turn for home repairs because there are not enough volunteers to get the work done.

ASP’s Year-Round/Adult/College program operates during all 12 months of the year.

Options for service include a few days, a week, or longer.

Construction/building experience is not required, however skilled workers are highly beneficial. ASP matches the skill level of groups with project requirements.

Everyone can help.
